Beef Stew with Chapati

A hearty and flavorful East African dish that combines tender beef stew with freshly made chapati flatbread. Perfect for warming up on cool evenings.

Ingredients

For Beef Stew:

  • 500g beef, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • 3 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 potatoes, cubed
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups beef stock or water
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

For Chapati:

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1 cup warm water
  • Extra oil for cooking

Instructions

For Beef Stew:

  1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat.
  2. Add beef and brown on all sides.
  3. Add onions, garlic, and ginger. Sauté until onions are translucent.
  4. Add tomatoes and cook until soft.
  5. Add curry powder, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper. Stir well.
  6. Pour in beef stock or water and bring to a boil.
  7. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 45 minutes.
  8. Add carrots and potatoes. Cook for another 20-30 minutes until vegetables are tender.
  9. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.

For Chapati:

  1. In a large bowl, mix flour and salt.
  2. Add oil and mix with your fingers until it resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Gradually add warm water while kneading to form a soft dough.
  4. Knead for about 10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  5. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes.
  6. Divide the dough into 8-10 equal portions.
  7. Roll each portion into a thin circle.
  8. Heat a flat pan over medium heat.
  9. Cook each chapati for 1-2 minutes on each side, adding a few drops of oil.
  10. Serve hot with beef stew.
